About the Crane Finder Tool


The Crane Finder Tool was built as a class project by Seth Frazier, Kirk McPhail, and Dorn Moore; students in the University of Wisconsin Online Master's Program in GIS & Web Map Programming in August 2017.

We gathered data from the Cornell Lab of Ornithology's eBird Online database of volunteered sightings of birds in the United States of America from January 2007 - December 2016. We also collected data from the U.S. Geological Survey's Protected Areas Database - PADUS - which includes data from around the USA of both public and private protected areas.

We combined these data to build the Crane Finder tool to help users find publicly accessible protected areas where they can see cranes. The database highlights the critical role that public and private protected areas play in ensuring a future for cranes in the USA.

The International Crane Foundation works worldwide to conserve cranes and the ecosystems, watersheds, and flyways on which they depend. They provide knowledge, leadership, and inspiration to engage people in resolving threats to cranes and their diverse landscapes. To encourage users of the Crane Finder application to share how cranes have inspired them, we asked users to share their stories. These stories help connect people from diverse areas and backgrounds through cranes.
