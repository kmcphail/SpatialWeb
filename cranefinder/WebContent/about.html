About the Crane Finder AP


The Crane Finder App was built as a class project by Seth Frazier, Kirk McPhail, and Dorn Moore; students in the University of Wisconsin Online Master's Program in GIS & Web Map Programming.

We gathered data from the Cornell Lab of Ornithology's eBird Online database of volunteered sightings of birds in the United States of America from 2007 - 2016. We also collected data from the U.S. Geological Survey's Protected Areas Database - PADUS - which includes data from around the USA of both public and private protected areas.

We combined these datasets to build this app to help users find publicly accessible protected areas where they can see cranes. The database highlights the critical role that public and private protected areas play in ensuring a future for cranes in the USA.
